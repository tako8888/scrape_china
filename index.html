How the Chinese Internet sees "Nuclear Waste Water" from Japan

Panic has gone, Public Awaits Rational Information

By YAMAKI, Takayuki

The Chinese internet is mysterious. They are isolated from other areas of the world by the so-called 'Great Firewall'. Also, they are not willing to open their information to the outside world, like through documented APIs.

Xiaohongshu (小红书) is one of the mysterious Chinese platforms. According to Statista, it has 450 million registered users. This website is primarily a social network based on pictures and videos and is much like Instagram, which cannot be accessed from China. Despite the huge number of users, Xiaohongshu is not well-known outside of China.

What is going on Xiaohongshu? I examine it by choosing a keyword "核废水"("Nuclear waste water"). "Nuclear waste water" is what chinese people call water from one Japanese nuculear plant "Fukushima Daiichi", which experienced severe nuclear disaster 10 years ago. The government of Japan insists the water is safe and IAEA, agrees with it. However, Chinese people don't beleieve so.

I enetered "核废水" to the search box of Xiaohongshu, and scraped about 220 posts from recent.
